# Command Center

> This feature requires the Fleet plan or higher.

The command center is your real-time operations dashboard, providing a unified view of your entire fleet's activity. It is the default landing page for dispatchers on Fleet and Freight Force plans.

## Fleet Map

The centerpiece of the command center is the live fleet map. Every driver with an active route or load appears on the map with a color-coded marker:

- **Green**: In transit, on schedule.
- **Yellow**: At a stop (pickup, delivery, rest, or fuel).
- **Red**: Alert condition (off-route, delayed, HOS issue).
- **Gray**: Off duty or no active assignment.

Click any driver marker to open a quick-view panel showing the driver's name, current load, next stop, ETA, and HOS summary. From this panel, you can open the full load or driver detail, or navigate to the route plan.

## Active Loads Panel

The active loads panel lists all loads currently in transit. Each entry shows the load reference, customer, driver, current location, and ETA to the next stop. Sort by ETA to identify upcoming deliveries, or filter by customer or driver.

## Alert Ticker

The alert ticker displays recent alerts requiring attention. Alerts scroll in order of priority and recency. Click any alert to view its detail and take action (acknowledge or resolve). The ticker count badge shows how many unacknowledged alerts are pending.

## Quick Actions

The command center supports rapid operational responses:

- **Click a driver on the map**: View details, see their route, or jump to their load.
- **Click an active load**: View load detail, update status, or contact the driver.
- **Click an alert in the ticker**: Open the alert for acknowledgment or resolution.
- **Filter by region**: Zoom into a geographic area to focus on local operations.
- **Filter by status or priority**: Narrow the view to only what needs attention.

## Using the Command Center Effectively

The command center works best as a persistent view on a dedicated monitor during operating hours. The map and alert ticker update in real-time, so you always see the latest fleet state. For smaller operations, check in periodically throughout the day rather than monitoring continuously.
