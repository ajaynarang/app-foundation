# Close Out and Reconciliation

Close out is the period-end process of ensuring every delivered load is fully reconciled — invoiced, documented, and paid. It gives you a clear picture of outstanding items and overall profitability.

## Accessing Close Out

Navigate to **Close Out** in the sidebar. The close-out page displays all delivered loads grouped by their reconciliation status.

## Reconciliation Statuses

Each delivered load is tracked against four completion criteria:

- **Needs Invoice**: The load has been delivered but no invoice has been generated. Click the load to generate an invoice.
- **Needs POD**: Proof of delivery has not been uploaded. Follow up with the driver or customer to obtain the signed POD.
- **Needs Payment**: An invoice has been sent but payment has not been received. Track aging and follow up on overdue invoices.
- **Complete**: Invoice generated, POD collected, payment received. This load is fully reconciled.

## Working Through Close Out

1. Review the **Needs Invoice** list. Click each load to generate and send invoices.
2. Review the **Needs POD** list. Contact drivers or customers to obtain missing proof of delivery documents.
3. Review the **Needs Payment** list. Follow up on outstanding invoices, especially those past the payment terms.
4. As each item is addressed, loads move to **Complete** automatically.

## Profitability View

The close-out page includes a profitability view showing revenue vs. cost per load. Revenue comes from the load rate and any additional charges. Costs include driver pay, fuel, tolls, and other expenses. This view helps identify your most and least profitable loads and lanes.

Overdue items are highlighted so you can prioritize collection efforts on aging receivables.

## Closing a Period

Once all loads in a date range are fully reconciled (or exceptions are documented), you can close the period. Closing a period locks those loads from further billing changes and provides a clean financial snapshot for that time frame.
