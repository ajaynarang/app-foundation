# Customer Portal

The customer portal provides your customers with self-service access to their shipment information, tracking, documents, and invoices — without exposing any of your internal fleet operations.

## What Customers Can Access

When a customer logs into the portal, they see only their own data:

- **Shipments**: A list of all loads booked by or for this customer, with current status (booked, in transit, delivered). Customers can filter by date, status, or reference number.
- **Real-time tracking**: Active shipments show live tracking with the current position of the truck and estimated delivery time. Tracking links can also be shared via email without requiring a portal login.
- **Documents**: Downloadable documents associated with their loads — BOL (Bill of Lading), POD (Proof of Delivery), and rate confirmations. Documents are available as soon as they are uploaded by your team.
- **Invoices**: All invoices issued to the customer, with status (sent, paid, overdue) and the ability to download PDF copies.

## What Customers Cannot Access

The portal strictly isolates customer data from your operations. Customers cannot see:

- Other customers' shipments or data
- Driver names, contact information, or personal details
- Vehicle information or fleet composition
- Financial data (your rates with other customers, driver pay, margins)
- Internal notes, alerts, or operational details

## Setting Up Customer Portal Access

1. Navigate to **Fleet → Customers** and open the customer record.
2. Ensure the customer has a valid **email address** on file.
3. Click **Invite to Portal**. The customer receives an email with instructions to set up their login.
4. The customer creates their own password and can then log in to the portal at any time.

## Managing Portal Access

To revoke portal access, open the customer record and click **Deactivate Portal Access**. The customer will no longer be able to log in, but their historical data remains in your system.
