# Voice Mode

> This feature requires the Fleet plan or higher.

Voice mode lets you interact with Sally using spoken language. Sally transcribes your speech, processes the request, and responds with voice — enabling hands-free operation for drivers on the road or dispatchers working with their hands full.

## Activating Voice Mode

1. Open Sally by clicking the **chat icon** or pressing **Cmd+K**.
2. Click the **microphone icon** in the Sally chat input area.
3. Grant microphone permission if prompted by your browser or device.
4. Begin speaking. Sally shows a visual indicator that she is listening.

## Using Voice Mode

Speak naturally as you would to a colleague. Voice mode supports all the same questions and actions as text chat:

- **Questions**: "What's my next stop?", "How many hours do I have left?", "Show me overdue invoices."
- **Actions** (Fleet+): "Assign load 1234 to Driver Smith.", "Mark load 5678 as delivered."
- **Follow-ups**: "Which of those are in transit?", "Send the invoice."

Sally transcribes your speech into text (visible in the chat window), processes the request, and responds with spoken audio. The text of her response also appears in the chat for reference.

## Deactivating Voice Mode

- Say **"Stop listening"** to deactivate voice mode.
- Click the **microphone icon** again to toggle it off.
- Close the Sally chat window.

## Tips for Voice Mode

- Speak clearly and at a normal pace. Sally handles natural speech patterns, including pauses and filler words.
- For load numbers and reference numbers, speak each digit individually (e.g., "load one two three four" rather than "load twelve thirty-four") for more accurate transcription.
- In noisy environments, bring the device closer or use a headset for better recognition.
- Voice mode works best with a stable internet connection, as audio is processed in real-time.

## Driver Use Case

Voice mode is particularly valuable for drivers. Instead of typing on a phone while parked, drivers can ask Sally questions and report updates hands-free:

- "What's my next stop?"
- "When is my next break?"
- "I've arrived at the pickup."
- "There's a delay at the dock."
