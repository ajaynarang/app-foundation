# Asking Sally Questions

Sally responds to natural language questions about your fleet data and how to use the SALLY platform. All plans include Sally chat.

## Opening Sally

- Click the **chat icon** in the bottom-right corner of any page.
- Press **Cmd+K** (Mac) or **Ctrl+K** (Windows) to open the Sally dialog.

The chat window opens over your current view. You can continue working while the chat is open.

## Types of Questions

### Data Questions

Ask about your fleet's current state and history. Sally queries your live database:

- "Show me all in-transit loads"
- "What loads are assigned to Driver Smith?"
- "What's the outstanding balance for ABC Freight?"
- "How many loads did we deliver last week?"
- "What's Driver Johnson's HOS status?"
- "Which invoices are overdue?"

Sally returns results with relevant details and links to the corresponding records in SALLY.

### Product Help Questions

Ask how to use features or accomplish tasks. Sally pulls answers from the product manual:

- "How do I create a load?"
- "How do I connect Samsara?"
- "What's the difference between the Fleet and Haul plans?"
- "How do I set up driver pay structures?"
- "How do I generate a settlement?"

Sally provides step-by-step guidance drawn from this documentation.

### Combination Questions

Sally handles questions that combine data lookups with guidance:

- "Load 1234 is delivered — what do I need to do next?" (Sally checks the load status and guides you through invoicing and close-out)
- "Driver Smith's CDL expires soon — how do I update it?" (Sally checks the expiry date and walks you through the update process)

## Conversation Context

Sally maintains conversation context within your session. You can ask follow-up questions:

1. "Show me loads for ABC Freight" — Sally returns the list.
2. "Which of those are overdue?" — Sally filters the previous results.
3. "Generate an invoice for load 1234" — Sally initiates the action (Fleet+ only).

Starting a new session clears the conversation history.

## Tips for Better Results

- Be specific: "What's the status of load 1234?" works better than "Tell me about my loads."
- Use names and numbers: Sally matches driver names, customer names, load references, and invoice numbers.
- Ask one thing at a time for the clearest responses.
