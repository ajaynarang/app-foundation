# Glossary

Key terms and acronyms used throughout the SALLY platform and the trucking industry.

## A

**Accessorial**: An additional charge beyond the base freight rate for services such as liftgate, inside delivery, or residential pickup.

## B

**BOL (Bill of Lading)**: A shipping document issued by the carrier confirming receipt of freight for shipment. Serves as a contract between shipper and carrier.

## C

**CDL (Commercial Driver's License)**: A license required to operate commercial motor vehicles. SALLY tracks CDL numbers and expiration dates for compliance.

**Close-Out**: The period-end reconciliation process in SALLY where delivered loads are verified for complete invoicing, POD collection, and payment receipt.

**Command Center**: SALLY's real-time operations dashboard showing the fleet map, active loads, and alert ticker. Requires Fleet plan.

## D

**Dead-Head**: Miles driven without a load (empty miles). Represents a cost without revenue, reducing profitability.

**Detention**: A charge assessed when a driver waits at a pickup or delivery facility beyond the agreed free time window. Compensates the carrier for lost productivity.

**DOT Number**: A unique number assigned by the U.S. Department of Transportation for identification and safety tracking of commercial carriers.

## E

**ELD (Electronic Logging Device)**: A device that automatically records a driver's driving time and hours of service. SALLY integrates with Samsara ELDs. Required by FMCSA for most commercial carriers.

## F

**Factoring**: The practice of selling invoices to a third-party company (a factor) for immediate cash at a discount, rather than waiting for the customer to pay.

**FMCSA (Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration)**: The federal agency within the U.S. Department of Transportation that regulates the trucking industry, including HOS rules, safety ratings, and carrier registration.

## G

**Geofence**: A virtual geographic boundary defined around a location. SALLY can generate alerts when vehicles enter or leave a geofenced area.

## H

**HOS (Hours of Service)**: Federal regulations that limit how long commercial drivers can drive and be on duty. Key limits: 11-hour driving, 14-hour duty window, 30-minute break after 8 hours, 70-hour/8-day cycle.

## I

**In Transit**: The load status indicating that the driver has departed the first pickup and is en route to the next stop.

## L

**Lane**: A recurring shipping route between a specific origin and destination. Carriers often quote rates per lane.

**Lumper**: A fee charged for loading or unloading freight at a facility, typically performed by third-party labor.

## M

**MC Number (Motor Carrier Number)**: A number issued by FMCSA that grants authority to operate as a for-hire carrier in interstate commerce.

**MCP (Model Context Protocol)**: An open standard for connecting AI assistants to external tools and data sources. SALLY exposes fleet management tools via MCP for use with compatible AI clients.

## P

**POD (Proof of Delivery)**: A document signed by the recipient confirming that freight was delivered successfully. Required for invoicing and close-out.

## R

**Rate-Con (Rate Confirmation)**: A contract between a carrier and a shipper or broker that specifies the rate, pickup/delivery details, and terms for a load. SALLY's document intelligence can auto-extract data from rate-con PDFs.

## S

**Settlement**: The calculation of a driver's pay for a given period. Includes gross earnings from loads, minus deductions (advances, fuel cards, insurance), resulting in net pay.

**Shield**: SALLY's compliance scoring and auditing engine. Evaluates fleet compliance posture across driver credentials, vehicle documentation, and operational practices. Requires Fleet plan.

**Stop**: A location on a route or load — can be a pickup, delivery, rest stop, or fuel stop. Each stop has an address, type, and time window.

## T

**Tenant**: An isolated organizational workspace in SALLY. All data (drivers, vehicles, loads, invoices) belongs to a tenant and is invisible to other tenants.

**TONU (Truck Ordered Not Used)**: A fee charged when a carrier dispatches a truck to a pickup but the load is cancelled by the shipper or broker.

**TSP/VRP (Travelling Salesman Problem / Vehicle Routing Problem)**: Mathematical optimization problems that SALLY solves to find the most efficient stop sequence and route for drivers, minimizing time or cost while respecting constraints.
