A 50-truck fleet exceeds the 25-truck limit on SALLY's Fleet plan, which means you are in Freight Force territory with custom pricing based on your fleet size, integration requirements, and support needs. For a tailored quote, contact sally@appshore.in with your truck count and a brief description of your operation. What follows is the ROI case for why the investment pays for itself many times over, regardless of the specific per-truck rate.

The biggest cost driver in most fleets is inefficient routing and empty miles. Fleets that move from manual dispatch to AI-optimized route planning typically see a 10 to 15 percent reduction in total miles driven. For a 50-truck operation where each truck runs 100,000 miles a year at roughly $1.80 per mile in fuel and wear, that translates to a substantial six-figure annual savings in operating costs alone. The route planning engine does not just shorten routes — it sequences stops optimally, avoids unnecessary toll roads, and inserts fuel stops at the cheapest stations, compounding the savings across every load.

HOS violations carry an average fine of $16,000 per incident and can trigger federal audits that threaten your operating authority. Fleets running 50 trucks without automated HOS validation typically accumulate 3 to 5 violations per year through honest scheduling mistakes. SALLY's HOS simulation catches every one of these during route planning, and continuous monitoring provides a second safety net during active routes. The compliance savings alone — measured in avoided fines, avoided audit costs, and preserved insurance rates — often exceed the platform cost by a wide margin.

Dispatcher efficiency improves dramatically when route planning goes from a 45-minute manual process to a few clicks. At 50 trucks, this time savings typically equates to one or two fewer full-time dispatchers needed, or alternatively, the same team handling significantly more volume. Automated invoicing with detention calculation, settlement generation with configurable pay structures, and close-out workflows eliminate hours of weekly administrative work. On-time delivery rates typically improve 15 to 20 percent, which translates directly to better customer retention, fewer penalty charges, and more favorable rate negotiations. The Freight Force plan also includes QuickBooks sync and external TMS integration, eliminating the double data entry that plagues operations running multiple disconnected systems. For a fleet your size, the question is not whether SALLY pays for itself — it is how quickly.
