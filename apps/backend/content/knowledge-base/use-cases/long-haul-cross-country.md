Consider a dispatcher who needs to move a load from Los Angeles to New York with three intermediate stops — a common long-haul scenario covering over 2,800 miles that will take a solo driver four to five days. This is exactly the kind of route where manual planning breaks down and SALLY's operations suite earns its keep. You will need at least the Fleet plan ($49/truck/month) to access route planning, HOS simulation, and continuous monitoring.

Without SALLY, the dispatcher spends 45 minutes or more building the route in a spreadsheet or on a paper map, manually checking the driver's HOS log to estimate where breaks need to happen, guessing which truck stops have parking at 10 PM on a Tuesday, and hoping fuel prices along I-40 have not spiked since last week. The driver departs, hits unexpected construction near Amarillo that adds two hours, and now the carefully planned rest stop timing is off. Near the Ohio border, the driver realizes he is 20 minutes short on his 30-minute break requirement and has to pull over at a random rest area with no services. If he miscalculates, that is a $16,000 HOS violation.

With SALLY, the dispatcher creates the load with all four stops, assigns a driver and vehicle, and clicks Plan Route. In seconds, SALLY produces an optimized stop sequence, simulates the full HOS clock across every segment (11-hour driving, 14-hour duty, 30-minute break, 70-hour cycle), and inserts rest stops at truck stops that match the driver's preferences. Fuel stops are placed at the cheapest stations within detour range, saving money on every fill-up across a route that will burn hundreds of gallons. Once the driver departs, continuous monitoring takes over — checking conditions every 60 seconds across all 14 trigger types. When that Amarillo construction delay hits, SALLY detects the ETA drift, recalculates the HOS impact, moves the next rest stop earlier if needed, and alerts the dispatcher with a clear summary of what changed and what action to take. The driver arrives in New York on time, with zero violations, and the dispatcher generates the invoice with one click.
