Fuel is typically a fleet's second-largest expense after driver pay, and SALLY's fuel optimization engine helps you spend less on every route. Available on Fleet ($49/truck/month) and Freight Force plans as part of the route planning suite, fuel optimization uses real data rather than guesswork to decide where your drivers should fill up.

The system starts with each vehicle's actual specs — tank capacity, current fuel level, and miles per gallon — to calculate exactly how far the truck can go before it needs fuel. When a route segment would consume more fuel than is available in the tank, SALLY searches for nearby fuel stops and compares real-time prices across stations within a configurable detour radius. The engine then inserts stops at the cheapest locations that keep the driver on schedule, factoring in the time cost of the detour against the fuel savings. Dispatchers see a complete fuel cost projection before the driver departs, including station names, estimated gallons needed, per-gallon prices, and the total projected fuel spend for the entire route.

The optimization is not a one-time calculation. If fuel prices change significantly after the route goes active, or if the route is re-planned due to a traffic delay or HOS adjustment, the fuel stops are re-evaluated automatically. This means your drivers are always directed to the best option available given current conditions, not the best option that existed when the route was originally planned. Over the course of a year, even saving a few cents per gallon on every fill-up adds up to meaningful cost reduction across a fleet — and SALLY does the comparison shopping automatically so no one has to check fuel price apps or make phone calls.
