Hours of Service violations cost carriers an average of $16,000 per incident in fines and can trigger federal audits that put your operating authority at risk. SALLY eliminates this exposure by validating HOS compliance at every stage of operations — during route planning, during active monitoring, and during dynamic re-planning. Available on Fleet ($49/truck/month) and Freight Force plans, HOS validation is deeply integrated into the route planning engine and monitoring service rather than being a standalone check.

During route planning, SALLY's HOS simulation engine models the driver's clock across every segment of the proposed route. It enforces the 11-hour driving limit, 14-hour on-duty window, mandatory 30-minute break after 8 consecutive hours of driving, 10-hour minimum off-duty rest period, and the 60/70-hour rolling weekly cycle. When the simulation detects that a driver will hit any limit before reaching the next stop, it automatically inserts the appropriate rest period. SALLY is smart about this — it evaluates whether a full 10-hour rest is needed, whether a split sleeper berth strategy would keep the driver moving sooner, or whether dock time already scheduled at a facility can count toward rest requirements. The result is a plan that is guaranteed compliant before the driver ever turns the key.

Once the route is active, HOS validation continues in real time. If your fleet uses Samsara ELD devices (available with Fleet plan's Samsara integration), SALLY pulls live HOS data and compares it against the plan. If actual driving hours diverge from projections — say the driver hit traffic and burned more clock than expected — the monitoring service recalculates and alerts the dispatcher if a rest stop needs to be moved earlier. All HOS decisions are logged with detailed reasoning, creating an audit trail that demonstrates proactive compliance management to FMCSA auditors.
