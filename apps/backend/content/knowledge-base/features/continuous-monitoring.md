SALLY's continuous monitoring service watches every active route around the clock, checking 14 distinct trigger types so dispatchers can stop reacting to problems and start preventing them. Available on Fleet ($49/truck/month) and Freight Force plans, this is the operational intelligence layer that turns SALLY from a record-keeping system into a proactive fleet management platform.

The 14 triggers span five categories. HOS triggers fire when a driver's driving hours or duty window approach their limits, giving dispatchers time to intervene before a violation occurs. Location triggers detect when a driver has not moved for an unusual period, departs late from a stop, goes off-route, enters or exits a geofence, or makes an unauthorized stop. Timing triggers catch dock delays where a driver sits longer than expected at a facility and flag when ETAs drift beyond acceptable thresholds. Safety triggers respond to speeding events, low fuel levels, breakdowns, and maintenance-due alerts from connected telematics. External triggers react to weather alerts and traffic congestion that could affect delivery timelines.

When any trigger fires, the monitoring service evaluates whether the situation requires a full route re-plan or just an ETA update. A severe weather event on the planned route might trigger a complete re-optimization with alternate highways, while a minor dock delay might simply push ETAs forward by thirty minutes. Either way, the dispatcher is notified through the alert system in the command center with a clear description of what happened, what SALLY did about it, and what action the dispatcher should take. The system checks conditions every 60 seconds for active routes, which means problems surface in under a minute rather than whenever someone happens to call the driver.
