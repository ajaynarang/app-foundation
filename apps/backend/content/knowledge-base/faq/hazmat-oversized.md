Yes, SALLY supports a full range of commodity types and equipment requirements across all plans. When creating a load, you specify the commodity type — general freight, hazmat, oversized, refrigerated, or other categories — along with any special handling instructions. Equipment type matching ensures the right vehicle is assigned: dry van, flatbed, reefer, tanker, step deck, and other configurations are all supported, so dispatchers can filter available vehicles by what the load actually requires. On the Fleet plan ($49/truck/month) and above, the route planning engine factors equipment and commodity constraints into optimization, ensuring routes comply with DOT regulations for special cargo and that the assigned vehicle can handle the load's requirements. Weight considerations, special permits, and restricted route segments are part of the planning calculus. On the Haul plan, commodity types and equipment matching are available through the core load management system — dispatchers specify requirements and assign vehicles manually, which gives full control without the automated route optimization layer.
